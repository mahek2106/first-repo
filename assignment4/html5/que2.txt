What are the additional tags used in HTML5?

--> Additional Tags in HTML5 : 1) <header> - Represents the header section of a page or section (like a logo or navigation).
                               2) <footer> - Defines the footer for a section or page (like copyrights or contact info).
                               3) <nav> - Used for navigation links.
                               4) <article> - Represents independent, self-contained content (like blog posts or news articles).
                               5) <section> - Defines a section of content, typically with a heading.
                               6) <aside> - Contains side content like ads, tips, or sidebars.
                               7) <main> - Specifies the main content of a document (only one per page).
                               8) <figure> - Used to group media elements like images with captions.
                               9) <figcaption> - Captions for the content inside <figure>.
                               10) <mark> - Highlights or marks text (like a highlighter).
                               11) <time> - Represents a date, time, or both.
                               12) <progress> - Displays the progress of a task (like a loading bar).
                               13) <meter> - Shows a measurement within a known range (e.g., a score or battery level).
                               14) <summary> - Used with <details>, provides a visible heading that can be clicked to expand/collapse content.
                               15) <details> - Creates a collapsible content section.
                               16) <audio> - Embeds sound content.
                               17) <video> - Embeds video content.
                               18) <canvas> - Used to draw graphics via JavaScript.
                               19) <svg> - Scalable Vector Graphics, for drawing shapes and images.
                               20) <output> - Displays the result of a calculation or script.
